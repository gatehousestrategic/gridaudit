FAQ
Questions we get a lot
How it works
You upload a utility bill — PDF, JPG, or PNG. Our AI reads every line item, identifies your utility provider and current rate schedule, then cross-references it against the published tariff schedules for your state. We flag anything that doesn't match: wrong rate class, incorrect demand calculations, taxes you shouldn't be paying, math errors, and more. The whole process takes under 60 seconds.
Most audits complete in 30–60 seconds. Occasionally a complex bill with many line items or an unusual rate structure takes up to 2 minutes. Either way, it's faster than anything a human auditor could do — and just as thorough.
We audit electric, natural gas, water/sewer, oil, and propane bills. We work with commercial and institutional accounts — skilled nursing facilities, assisted living, multifamily residential, manufacturing plants, commercial buildings, and retail. We do not currently audit residential consumer accounts.
All 50 states. Utility tariff rules vary significantly by state and even by individual utility within a state. Our system accounts for these differences — a healthcare facility in New York faces different rate classifications than one in Texas, and we handle both.
The savings
It depends on your facility size, utility type, and what errors are present. Our members recover an average of $4,200 per year per facility. SNFs and multifamily buildings with demand charges tend to see the largest recoveries — often $300–$800 per month. Even a modest finding on a single bill can more than cover a year of membership fees.
In most states, utilities are required to issue credits or refunds going back 12 to 36 months when billing errors are identified and disputed. The retroactive window varies by state and utility. Our full audit reports specify exactly how far back a claim can reach, and our dispute letters are written to maximize the recovery period.
Rate misclassification — being billed under the wrong tariff schedule for your facility type. It's the single most common and most costly error. Utilities assign rate schedules when an account is first opened and rarely revisit them, even as a facility's usage profile changes or new, more favorable rates become available. Healthcare facilities are especially susceptible because many states offer specific rate classes for medical facilities that utilities don't proactively apply.
We make it as easy as possible. Your full audit report includes a ready-to-send dispute letter addressed to your utility's billing department. It cites the specific tariff provisions, identifies the errors, and formally requests a review and credit. Most customers send it by certified mail or email — no negotiation required. If a utility pushes back, we can help you escalate through your state's public utilities commission.
Pricing & membership
Traditional utility auditors charge 20–40% of whatever they recover — on a $5,000 annual finding, that's $1,000–$2,000 going to the auditor, every year. With GridAudit, you pay a flat monthly fee and keep every dollar you recover. For most facilities, a single finding pays for multiple years of membership. We think that's a much better deal — and AI makes it possible.
Your audit counter resets at the start of each billing month. If you need more audits before then, you can upgrade your plan at any time and the new limit takes effect immediately. For large one-time needs — like auditing an entire portfolio at once — contact us and we'll work something out.

Yes. Bill files are transmitted over encrypted connections and stored in a private, access-controlled storage bucket. Only your account can access your bills and audit results. We do not sell or share your data with third parties, and we do not use your bill data to train AI models.
We specialize in commercial and institutional accounts with complex rate structures: skilled nursing facilities (SNFs), assisted living communities, multifamily apartment buildings, manufacturing plants, warehouses, commercial offices, and retail. These facility types tend to have the most errors and the highest savings potential. We do not audit residential consumer accounts.
